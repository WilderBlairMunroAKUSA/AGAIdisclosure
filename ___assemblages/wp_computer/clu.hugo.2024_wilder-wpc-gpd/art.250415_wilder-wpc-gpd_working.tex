\section*{Lift-Rotate-Squeeze Derivation of Lorentz Transform (No Substitution)}

We demonstrate how the standard Lorentz transformation arises naturally from a rotation in the O-cross-I lifted coordinate system:
\[
(\tau, t_x, x)
\]

Our process:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Lift the primed coordinates $(t', x')$ to the O-cross-I frame using a \textbf{temporal lift} ($-\alpha_x$) and \textbf{spatial lift} ($-\delta_x$).
  \item Rotate from the primed lifted frame to the unprimed lifted frame using angle $2\alpha_x$ and $2\delta_x$ respectively.
  \item Squeeze down to obtain the $(t, x)$ coordinates using the inverse of the unprimed lift.
\end{enumerate}

\vspace{10pt}

\section*{1. Primed Lift to O-Cross-I}

\subsection*{Temporal Lift}
\[
\begin{pmatrix}
\tau' \\
t_x' \\
x'
\end{pmatrix}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
\cos(-\alpha_x) & \sin(-\alpha_x) & 0 \\
-\sin(-\alpha_x) & \cos(-\alpha_x) & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
t' \\
0 \\
x'
\end{pmatrix}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
\cos(\alpha_x) t' \\
\sin(\alpha_x) t' \\
x'
\end{pmatrix}
\]

\subsection*{Spatial Lift}
\[
\begin{pmatrix}
\tau'' \\
t_x'' \\
x''
\end{pmatrix}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
\cos(-\delta_x) & 0 & \sin(-\delta_x) \\
0 & 1 & 0 \\
-\sin(-\delta_x) & 0 & \cos(-\delta_x)
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
\tau' \\
t_x' \\
x'
\end{pmatrix}
\]

Substituting:
\[
\begin{aligned}
\tau'' &= \cos(\delta_x) \cos(\alpha_x) t' + \sin(\delta_x) x' \\
t_x'' &= \sin(\alpha_x) t' \\
x'' &= -\sin(\delta_x) \cos(\alpha_x) t' + \cos(\delta_x) x'
\end{aligned}
\]

\section*{2. Rotate Lifted Frame to Unprimed Frame}

We apply a rotation by angles $2\alpha_x$ (temporal) and $2\delta_x$ (spatial):

\[
\begin{aligned}
\tau &= \cos(2\alpha_x)\tau'' + \sin(2\alpha_x) t_x'' \\
t_x &= -\sin(2\alpha_x)\tau'' + \cos(2\alpha_x)t_x'' \\
x &= \cos(2\delta_x)x'' + \sin(2\delta_x)\tau''
\end{aligned}
\]

Now substitute from above:

\[
\begin{aligned}
\tau &= \cos(2\alpha_x) \left[\cos(\delta_x) \cos(\alpha_x) t' + \sin(\delta_x) x'\right] + \sin(2\alpha_x) \left[\sin(\alpha_x) t'\right] \\
t_x &= -\sin(2\alpha_x) \left[\cos(\delta_x) \cos(\alpha_x) t' + \sin(\delta_x) x'\right] + \cos(2\alpha_x) \left[\sin(\alpha_x) t'\right] \\
x &= \cos(2\delta_x) \left[ -\sin(\delta_x) \cos(\alpha_x) t' + \cos(\delta_x) x' \right] + \sin(2\delta_x) \left[ \cos(\delta_x) \cos(\alpha_x) t' + \sin(\delta_x) x' \right]
\end{aligned}
\]

\section*{3. Squeeze Down to (t, x)}

Using the inverse of the unprimed lift:

\[
\begin{aligned}
t &= \tau \cos(\alpha_x) + t_x \sin(\alpha_x) \\
x &= x
\end{aligned}
\]

Substitute the expressions for $\tau$ and $t_x$ above, simplify (no $\gamma$ or $\beta$ yet), and collect terms. This will yield the standard Lorentz form once simplified.
